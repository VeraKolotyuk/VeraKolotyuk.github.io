Simplified adding and plan sections during signup

Tablet · SaaS signup

Context

The signup flow had to present plan tiers and optional add-ons on a tablet-first canvas. The risk was overwhelming new teams with configuration before they had seen value.

Analytics suggested drop-off clustered around plan comparison and add-on selection — people needed clarity, not more surface area.

Goals

The redesign focused on:

  • two clear plan tiers with honest comparison, not marketing noise
  • a single obvious primary action per viewport
  • add-ons collapsed behind an explicit “customize” path

The challenge was to keep revenue opportunities visible for buyers who need them while keeping the default path short for everyone else.

Approach

I used a dark canvas to stage the plans as tangible “plates”, then tightened typography and spacing until each tier read as a complete story without scrolling on common tablet heights.

The guiding constraint for layout and copy:

Every screen should answer which plan fits you — before it asks what else you might buy.

Iterations centered on:

  • progressive disclosure for add-ons and seat math
  • anchoring the recommended tier without hiding alternatives
  • error prevention for annual vs monthly and renewal dates

Stakeholder reviews validated that the shortened default path still surfaced upsell for teams that needed deeper configuration.

Outcomes

The flow now presents two clear plan tiers on a dark canvas with a single primary action per viewport, while add-ons sit behind a deliberate customize step so the default path stays short.

The layout system also translated cleanly to smaller breakpoints without reintroducing clutter.
