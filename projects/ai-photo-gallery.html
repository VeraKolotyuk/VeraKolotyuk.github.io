AI Smart Gallery

Web · Listing quality · AI-assisted UX

Context

Listings with weak or inconsistent photos underperform, yet manually curating large galleries is slow and subjective. The product explored AI-assisted selection and enhancement to lift quality without adding heavy cognitive load for hosts.

The interface had to serve two mindsets: visitors who skim a calm public gallery, and hosts who need a structured editor for categories, cover photos, and review of AI-generated layouts.

Goals

The work aimed to:

  • improve perceived listing quality through smarter defaults
  • pair a restrained gallery experience with a powerful editor
  • make AI suggestions reviewable and reversible, not “black box”

The challenge was to surface automation without eroding trust — hosts still needed to feel in control of what guests see.

Approach

I separated public-facing presentation from editing workflows, then connected them with explicit states for “draft”, “AI suggested”, and “published” so users always knew what was live.

The core principle for the experience:

AI should propose; the host should decide — in one calm surface, not a maze of modals.

Explorations focused on:

  • category and cover-photo rules that stay visible while scrolling long galleries
  • batch review patterns for AI layouts before they ship
  • microcopy that explains what changed and why

Visual language stayed quiet so photography stayed hero; controls recede until you need them.

Outcomes

The result pairs a calm public-facing gallery with a structured editor for categories, cover photos, and review of AI-generated layouts — improving listing quality while keeping hosts in control.

The system also established patterns that could extend to video and 3D tours later without breaking the mental model.
