ShowMojo mobile application

Mobile · B2B SaaS

Context

Users manage key operational workflows on the go: dense scheduling, threaded conversations, and quick actions that cannot afford mistakes. The previous mobile experience struggled to balance overview and focus.

Session-style usage meant people often opened the app between showings — they needed to answer “what changed?” and “what do I tap next?” in seconds.

Goals

The redesign targeted:

  • legible calendar density on small screens without losing context
  • chat and actions reachable with one hand
  • clear hierarchy between “now”, “soon”, and “later” work

The challenge was to serve power users who live in the product while still onboarding new managers who dip in weekly.

Approach

I mapped the highest-frequency tasks, then prototyped navigation and card patterns that keep scheduling and messaging one gesture apart instead of buried in tabs.

The product promise on mobile distilled to:

Let people resolve the next operational issue before they reach the car — without hunting for the right screen.

Design directions included:

  • time-first layouts that compress empty space but preserve anchors
  • focused chat surfaces with attachments and shortcuts visible in context
  • progressive disclosure for advanced filters and bulk edits

Visual treatment leaned on strong typographic rhythm and plate contrast so dense data still felt scannable outdoors.

Outcomes

The mobile application now balances dense scheduling views with focused chat and quick actions — helping users manage workflows away from the desk with more confidence.

The component set also made it easier to align tablet and desktop variants so patterns stay recognizable across breakpoints.
